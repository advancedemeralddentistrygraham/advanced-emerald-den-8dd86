Frequently Asked Questions About Endodontist

How much does a root canal typically cost at Advanced Emerald Dentistry in Graham, and does insurance cover it?
At Advanced Emerald Dentistry in Graham, the cost of a root canal generally ranges from $900 to $1,500, depending on the tooth's location and complexity of the procedure. Most dental insurance plans cover a significant portion of endodontic treatment; however, coverage amounts can vary. Our office team is happy to provide a detailed estimate based on your specific insurance plan and answer any questions you have about out-of-pocket costs.
How long does a root canal procedure take, and will I need multiple appointments?
Most root canal procedures at our Graham office are completed in one to two visits, with each appointment typically lasting 60 to 90 minutes. The exact timeline depends on the tooth's condition and whether the case is complex. After your initial consultation, we'll let you know what to expect for your specific situation.
Are your endodontists licensed and experienced in Washington state?
Yes, our endodontists at Advanced Emerald Dentistry are fully licensed in Washington state and have extensive experience performing root canals and other endodontic procedures. Our team participates in ongoing education to stay current with the latest techniques and technology in the field.
What is the process for getting a root canal at Advanced Emerald Dentistry?
The process starts with a consultation and digital X-rays to assess the tooth. If a root canal is needed, we'll numb the area, carefully remove the infected tissue, clean and shape the root canal, and then seal it. In most cases, a temporary or permanent filling is placed, and a crown may be recommended to protect the tooth. Our Graham team will explain every step and answer any questions you have along the way.
Do you offer any guarantees or warranties on your endodontic work?
At Advanced Emerald Dentistry, we stand behind the quality of our endodontic treatments. We offer a limited warranty on root canal procedures, which typically covers retreatment if a treated tooth fails within the first year (provided proper aftercare and follow-up are maintained). We'll review the details of our warranty with you before your procedure so you know exactly what's covered.
